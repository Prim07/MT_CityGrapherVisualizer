CityGrapher & CityGrapher Visualizer 2020, AGH Univeristy of Science and Technology

Simulated annealing is a heuristic algorithm used for approximating global optimum of a given function. Mainly, it is recommended for problems where search space is too large to use a classic approach.

One of these problems is finding optimal distribution of local service centres in a city (on a city graph).

We created a tool which solves this problem. All you have to do is provide a city you are interested in and run algorithm. Just insert its name and number of requested results (i.e. number of crossings to find). You can also choose whether to draw or not all nodes and crossings on the map. You will be informed about the next stages of the process: data collecting and calculating.

For the smaller cities you can try running Brute Force algorithm. However, this option in available only for 1-3 requested nodes.

CityGrapher and CityGrapher Visualizer are BSc Thesises made by two students from AGH UST. The main goal of the project is to create an algorithm for finding an optimal distribution of local service centres on transportation network. The graph is created with real OpenStreetMaps city data and algorithm is based on the simulated annealing idea.
